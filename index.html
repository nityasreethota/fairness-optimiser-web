⚖ NHS Fairness Optimiser

Interactive implementation of optimal group-specific thresholds for NHS mental health triage based on Bayesian decision theory and Chouldechova's impossibility theorem.

Computes group-specific decision thresholds that minimise expected clinical harm while accounting for differences in data quality, prevalence, and the costs of clinical decisions.

Research Problem

This interactive application accompanies my research on Bayesian Fairness Optimisation for NHS clinical decision-making. Rather than applying a single decision threshold to every patient, the optimiser derives group-specific Bayesian decision thresholds that minimise expected clinical harm while accounting for different prevalence, unequal error costs and fairness constraints.

Cost-Sensitive Fair Threshold Optimisation

This application searches for decision thresholds that minimise expected classification loss while illustrating the fairness trade-offs described by Chouldechova (2017).

Expected Loss

For a classifier operating at threshold \(t\),

$$ L(t) = C_{FN}\, FNR(t)\, P(Y=1) + C_{FP}\, FPR(t)\, P(Y=0) $$

where

  • \(C_{FN}\) = false negative cost
  • \(C_{FP}\) = false positive cost
  • \(FNR\) = false negative rate
  • \(FPR\) = false positive rate

Group-specific Costs

The optimiser allows asymmetric costs for protected groups.

$$ C^{A}_{FN} = \lambda C_{FN} $$

where

$$ \lambda \ge 1 $$

is the user-selected multiplier.

Positive Predictive Value

$$ PPV = \frac{ TPR \times P(Y=1) } { TPR \times P(Y=1) + FPR \times P(Y=0) } $$

Chouldechova Impossibility

If two demographic groups have different base rates,

$$ P_A \ne P_B $$

then equalising all fairness metrics simultaneously is generally impossible.

Even if

$$ TPR_A=T P R_B $$

and

$$ FPR_A=FPR_B $$

the resulting PPVs become

$$ PPV_A \ne PPV_B $$

whenever

$$ P_A \ne P_B. $$

Interpreting the Results / Clinical Interpretation

Higher thresholds reduce false positives but increase false negatives.

Expected Loss identifies the operating point that minimises total decision cost.

ROC curves evaluate discrimination independently of prevalence.

PPV differences illustrate Chouldechova's impossibility theorem when base rates differ.

Changing the cost multiplier shifts the optimal threshold for the protected group.
